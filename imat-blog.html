IMAT 2025: Your Medical Journey Starts Here

Dreaming of studying medicine in Italy? Get the key dates, tips, and resources you need to conquer the IMAT.

1 | Overview of IMAT 2025

The International Medical Admissions Test (IMAT) is a paper-based entrance exam for English-taught medicine and dentistry programs at Italian public universities. The exam is scheduled for Wednesday, 17 September 2025, and will be conducted simultaneously across Italy and internationally.

  • 60 multiple-choice questions
  • 100 minutes
  • +1.5 points per correct answer
  • –0.4 points per incorrect answer

Sections:

  • General Knowledge & Reading Comprehension (4 questions)
  • Logical Reasoning (5 questions)
  • Biology (23 questions)
  • Chemistry (15 questions)
  • Physics and Mathematics (13 questions)

3 | What's New in 2025?
  • Confirmed Continuation: IMAT is not cancelled despite Italian medical reform. Changes apply only to Italian-language programs.
  • Still Paper-Based: No online version planned. Test remains single-day, in-person.
  • Managed by MUR: Since 2023, IMAT is no longer designed by Cambridge Assessment. MUR has maintained the structure but adjusted section weightings.

6 | How to Register

EU / EU-Equivalent Students:

  1. Sign up on universitaly.it
  2. Choose and rank universities
  3. Select your test centre (in Italy)
  4. Pay €130
  5. Download confirmation

Non-EU Students:

  1. Pre-enrol on universitaly.it by 31 July 2025
  2. Upload passport, diploma, translation + Declaration of Value or CIMEA verification
  3. Await university approval
  4. Register for IMAT (select one first-choice university only)
  5. Pay €130
  6. Choose international test centre

9 | FAQs
  • Is IMAT 2025 cancelled? No, it’s officially confirmed by MUR.
  • Can I take the test online? No, IMAT remains a paper-based in-person test.
  • Can I apply to more than one university? Yes, EU applicants can rank multiple. Non-EU candidates apply to ONE university only.
  • When will the registration open? Likely July 2025, exact date in MUR decree.
